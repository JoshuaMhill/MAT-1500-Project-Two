Trade In & Purchase Price

Trade In & Purchase Price

I don't own a car; however, I'm sure my mother-in-law in Los Angeles won't need hers, so I'll trade in her 2019 Lexus RX 350.
She owns the car outright meaning no loan transfers, and only has 22,000 miles on it.


Once the dealership confirms a trade-in price, we can start working out our financial details.

Image Source
Trade In & Purchase Price

According to Sunrise Chevy, the dealership will offer me a trade-in of $32,721 for this car in the condition that it is in.



Trade In & Purchase Price

If the dealership is offering us $32,721 for trade-in, that means I need to reevaluate the total cost.

Total Cost = 52,530-32,721.
Total cost = $19,809

I also need to apply Sales Tax which, in NYC, can be rather steep. Sales Tax in Queens County is 8.88%.Therefore:
Payment = Total Cost + Sales Tax;
Payment = 19,809 + (19,809 * 0.0888)
Payment = $21,568.04

So, after the trade-in is applied and Sales Tax is added, I will owe $21,568.04 for the total purchase price.

However, this is not the total amount I need to be financed.

Remember, Deposit=15% of Purchase Price. Therefore:

Deposit is 21,568.04*0.15 = $3,235.21

Loan Amount is 21,568.04-3,235.21 which is $18,332.83

Calculations

If I enter the approximate bank rate of 7% to the loan formula for 60 months (5 years) at ,onthly repayments:

At this payment rate, throughout the loan, I will pay:

Moving on, if we use the dealer financing rate of 8.6% in the loan formula for 60 months at monthly repayments I calculate:

Conclusion

Conclusion

It is clear from the calculations that, if I can get the average APR from a bank for my credit score, that would be the best option. If I can, I will pay a total of $21,780 total back to the bank.
As the original price for the car was $18,332.83, I will pay (21,780.6 -18,332.83) or $3,447.77 total interest.


If I need to finance through the dealership, I will pay $22,620.6 to the dealership.
The principle is $18,332.83, meaning I will pay (22,620.6-18,332.83) or $4,287.77 total interest.


If I have to finance through an online lender, I will pay $23,316 to the lender.
The principle is the same at $18,332.83, so I will pay (23,365.8-18,332.83) or $5032.97 total interest.

Conclusion
